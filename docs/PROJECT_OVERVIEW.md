# Anbaro: Project Overview and Creative Brief

## The short version

**Anbaro is a simple, free inventory-management app for small businesses.** It helps a team know what stock they have, where it is stored, and what is running low—without living in messy spreadsheets or paying for complicated enterprise software.

**Core message:** _Know what you have, where it is, and what is running low—without the spreadsheet chaos._

## What Anbaro is building

Anbaro gives small-business owners and their teams a calm, practical way to manage physical inventory. It is designed for the real places where stock is counted and moved: a shop floor, storeroom, walk-in cooler, supply closet, salon, warehouse, or office.

The vision is straightforward: inventory should not feel like an enterprise project. A small business should be able to set up its locations and items, count stock on a phone, check the results on a computer, and get a warning before something runs out. Everyone should work from the same up-to-date numbers.

## Why it was built

Anbaro was built by a solo builder to solve a common small-business problem: spreadsheets are familiar, but they become hard to trust as a business grows. They are easy to duplicate, forget to update, and difficult to use while standing in a stockroom. On the other end, enterprise inventory systems can be expensive, slow to set up, and overloaded with features a small team does not need.

Anbaro sits in the middle. It gives small teams the inventory basics that matter every day—clear stock levels, organized locations, simple counts, scan-to-find items, and low-stock alerts—without unnecessary complexity.

## Who it is for

Anbaro is for small businesses that keep physical stock and have outgrown an informal spreadsheet or paper-based process. It is useful for:

- Retail shops
- Restaurants and cafes
- Salons and studios
- Small warehouses and stockrooms
- Offices managing supplies
- Any team managing inventory across one or more locations

It is for owners, managers, and staff who need a shared, reliable view of stock without becoming inventory experts first.

## The main purpose

The main purpose of Anbaro is to help a small business avoid the costly surprises caused by unclear inventory:

- Running out of an item unexpectedly
- Ordering too late or buying more than needed
- Losing track of stock across different rooms or locations
- Spending hours fixing spreadsheet mistakes
- Not knowing why a stock number changed

Anbaro makes the basic inventory workflow clear: **add what you carry, record where it is, count it, see what is low, and decide what to reorder.**

## Key features

| Feature                    | What it means for a small business                                                                                                          |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Item and location tracking | See what items you have and the quantity at each location.                                                                                  |
| Guided stock counts        | Run repeatable counts that help staff count carefully and reconcile results.                                                                |
| Offline mobile counting    | Continue counting in a back room or other low-signal area; changes sync when the connection returns.                                        |
| Barcode scanning           | Use a phone camera to find or add an item quickly—no separate scanner hardware needed.                                                      |
| Low-stock alerts           | Get notified when an item reaches its chosen low-stock level.                                                                               |
| Reorder suggestions        | See suggested quantities based on target stock levels, then review the recommendation yourself. Anbaro does not place orders automatically. |
| Supplier details           | Keep supplier contact information and item relationships in one place.                                                                      |
| Units and pack sizes       | Track stock in useful units, such as pieces, weight, volume, cases, or packs.                                                               |
| Stock history              | Keep a clear record of stock movements and adjustments, so teams can understand how a quantity changed.                                     |
| Team access                | Invite staff and give people the access they need.                                                                                          |
| CSV import and export      | Bring in an existing catalog and take your data with you.                                                                                   |
| Web and mobile apps        | Review inventory at a desk and count it on the floor, with shared data.                                                                     |

## What makes it simple

Simplicity is a product principle, not just a marketing word. Anbaro is simple because it focuses on the daily questions a small team actually needs answered:

1. What do we have?
2. Where is it?
3. What is running low?
4. What should we count or reorder next?

It supports that workflow with plain-language screens, guided setup, mobile barcode scanning, and a clear view of items by location. Teams do not need special hardware, a long implementation project, or an operations specialist to get started.

Anbaro is still reliable beneath that simple surface. Changes are recorded in stock history, count results can be reviewed, and offline count work is protected until it can sync. The goal is a tool that feels light to use while giving a business numbers it can trust.

## Why it is free

Anbaro is currently free while it builds an audience. The goal is to remove the cost barrier that often keeps small businesses stuck with spreadsheets, paper, or an overcomplicated trial.

The free product is meant to be genuinely useful, not a temporary demo with basic inventory features locked away. The product is built with a path to transparent paid plans in the future, but the current message is simple: **Anbaro is free to use.** Do not imply a price, a trial deadline, feature restrictions, or a future pricing date unless those details have been confirmed separately.
